# Puritan Era
## Puritan
定义: the puritans were members of a **religious reform movement(宗教改革)** known as Puritanism that arose with in the Church of England in the late 16th century. They believe that each individual can communicate directly with God and advocate a simple and equal life for all believers before god, called *purification*
	 **清教主义的核心思想**

1. **预定论（Predestination）**  
    加尔文主义的核心教义，认为上帝已预先选定 “选民”（Elect）得救，人类无法通过善行改变命运，但可通过虔诚生活和世俗成就（如勤奋工作、道德自律）印证自己的选民身份。
    
2. **契约思想（Covenant Theology）**  
    强调人与上帝的 “契约” 关系：选民需遵守上帝律法，若违背则会招致惩罚。这一思想延伸至社会层面，形成《五月花公约》等早期殖民地契约，成为美国民主法治的思想源头之一。
    
3. **天职观（Calling）**  
    认为劳动和世俗职责是上帝赋予的 “天职”，勤奋工作、节俭自律是荣耀上帝的方式，奠定了美国资本主义伦理的基础。
    
4. **原罪与救赎**  
    强调人类与生俱来的原罪，需通过忏悔、信仰和遵行教义寻求救赎，这一观念渗透于美国文学的道德焦虑与救赎主题中。
## Puritanism
### 定义:
Puritanism covered a period from the 1550s to 1700. It originally developed in England to purify the church of the trappings of Catholicism.
### 8 basic puritan tenets(八条基本信条)
**1, God's absolute sovereignty(上帝的绝对主权)
2, Unconditional election(无条件筛选)
3, Original sin(原罪)
4, Total depravity(全然败坏)**
5, Limited atonement
6, Irresistible grace
7, The perseverance of the saints
8, Predestination
## Puritan literature
### 定义: 
Literature of colonial period can be regarded as puritan literature, because the body of writings were produced by the puritans.
### 影响: 
Emerging in the 17th century, Puritan literature was deeply influenced by the religious beliefs, social values, and historical context of the Puritans. Therefore, Puritan literature represents a significant and foundational aspect of American literary history.
### 代表作家:
Pioneer of colonial America:

### 写作目的:
to record the puritan's experience in the new world
to attract more Europeans to this new land.
to reinforce authority of the bible and church
to provide spiritual insight and instruction

### 内容与主题:
**American dream:** based on the biblical myth of the garden of eden, the settlers believed that through hard work, courage and determination, one can achieve a better life.
**Religious devotion
Moral and Didactic Content**
### 主要形式:
Personal narrative accounts: diaries, histories, bio & autobiography, letter, travel book, sermon
Poem: for serious self-examination
Non-fictional: No fiction and drama, for these are regarded as sinful
### 写作风格:
1, Under the influence of the bible , the language is fresh, simple and direct with a clear statement
2, the rhetoric is plain and honest with little figurative language, though there are frequent biblical allusions.
3, the puritan's metaphorical mode of perception. To them, the physical world was a symbol of God.

## 长远影响
1, Cultural Identity: emphasizing values such as **individualism, self-reliance**, which continue to be integral to America.
2, Religious and Moral Foundations: Puritan literature laid the **religious and moral foundations** of American literature. The themes of sin, redemption, divine providence, and the ethical life continued to be central to American writing.
3, Plain style and Clarity: preference for plain writing influenced many American writers, Benjamin Franklin for example.
4, Social and Political Impact: profound influence on American ideas of liberty, democracy, and the separation of church and state.

## Transcendentalism
### 定义
A literacy, philosophical and artistic movement that flourished in New England. It originated among a small group of intellectuals who were reacting against the orthodoxy of Calvinism and the rationalism of the Unitarian church, developing their own faith centering on the divinity of humanity and the natural world.
### 起源
1, Idealistic philosophy from Germany
2, Oriental mysticism; philosophies of Confucius and Mencius; Taoism
3, American Puritanism
### 特色
1, Emphasize spirit or the Oversoul as the most important thing in the universe
2, Relying on intuition and conscience
3, Nature as symbolic of spirit or God
4, Stress the importance of the individual

# Literature of Realism
## 定义
"Realism" was a late 19th-century literary movement (post-Civil War to early 20th century) opposing Romanticism's idealization. It aimed to depict ordinary life, people, and social realities objectively with detailed, accurate portrayals, focusing on psychological depth, environmental/social influences, and everyday struggles.
## 写作特点
1, Stress truthful treatment of material
2, Insist on precise description, authentic action and dialogue
3, represent contemporary life and everyday scenes in a straightforward or matter-of-fact manner
4, Emphasize objectivity and offer an objective view.
## 代表作家
William Dean Howells
Henry James
Mark Twain
## Local Colorism(地方特色主义)
### 特点:
relies heavily on using words, phrases, and slang that were native to the particular region in which the tory took place
### 代表作家:
Hamlin Garland
Bret Harte
Willa Cather
Mark Twain

### 写作特点:
1, writing truthfully and objectively
2, stress the animality of men who are dominated by the irresistible forces of evolution.
3, describe the lives of the downtrodden and the abnormal
